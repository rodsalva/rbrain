## Compiled Truth

### The Market Transition

The legal services industry is undergoing a fundamental shift driven by AI, specifically legal AI platforms like Harvey. Key dynamics observed as of late 2025:

**2023:** Firms resistant. AI adoption threatening billable hour revenue (document review generates most billable hours). Firms worried about margin compression.

**2024:** Inflection point. Adoption becomes inevitable — either competitors adopt AI or clients start using AI themselves and reduce outside counsel spend. Both pressures force law firms in.

**Now (2025):** Firms are thinking actively about business model transformation. Two tracks: (1) fixed-fee pricing for AI-commoditized tasks + premium billable for relationship/strategy work; (2) race to compress "time to partner" (how fast a junior becomes a full lawyer with strong client relationships).

### Employment Impact

- **No mass layoffs** — the narrative is more nuanced. Current pattern: **hiring freezes**, not cuts.
- Corporate in-house: split pattern — some reducing external counsel and hiring more in-house; others freezing headcount.
- Law firm side: some freezes starting as firms figure out AI workflows.
- Long-term hypothesis: legal market is **cyclical with the economy** — as GDP grows, transaction complexity increases, creating more legal work. AI enables same headcount to handle more complex, larger deal rooms.
- Example: Microsoft/Activision merger involved hundreds of partners across dozens of geographies. As M&A complexity grows, AI amplifies capacity rather than replacing it entirely.

### The Billable Hour Disruption

- Junior associate bills ~$1K/hour for work that could be done by a college student.
- Best M&A partner in a specialty: $2,500–$3,000/hour.
- This delta makes no sense when AI handles junior-level tasks. Resolution: junior work → fixed fee; senior partner work → premium pricing.
- Law firms that adapt fastest will capture business from those that don't.

### Burnout Reduction (Not Hours Reduction)

- AI will NOT reduce total hours worked. Lawyers will work the same hours.
- Burnout is driven by monotonous work, not hours per se. Law school (same long hours) doesn't burn people out — because the work is interesting and varied.
- AI eliminates the monotonous portion (document review, routine filings) → lawyers do more interesting work → less burnout at same hours.

### Customer Behavior

- Enterprise clients buying Harvey even when they already have ChatGPT/Claude enterprise licenses — co-existence validates domain-specific value.
- Users are "very loud" with feedback — creates moat from density of domain signal.
- Corporate side adopting Harvey internally and sending less work to outside counsel (structural shift in legal services distribution).

### Law School Partnerships (Demand Generation)

- Harvey partners with law schools (students already using ChatGPT anyway).
- Strategy: get students using Harvey before they enter firms → arrive at firms already trained.
- Analogy: Apple giving MacBooks + iPods to college freshmen (brand loyalty for life).
- Secondary value: law school use cases differ from practice → good product experimentation sandbox.
