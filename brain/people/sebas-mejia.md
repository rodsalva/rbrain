## Executive Summary

Sebastián Mejia is a Colombian serial entrepreneur, currently CEO of Tako (AI workforce management for Brazil/LATAM). Before Tako, co-founded Rappi in 2015 — the first delivery super app in Latin America, first Colombian unicorn, operating across 9 countries and 300 cities. Left Rappi ~2024 to start Tako. US citizen, Colombian-born, raised across Latin America and Europe. Known for relentless focus on defensibility, building in hard markets, and recruiting globally elite talent.

## State

- **Current:** CEO of Tako (AI labor/workforce platform for Brazil/LATAM)
- **Previous:** Co-founder of Rappi (stepped down ~2024)
- **Based:** San Francisco / Brazil
- **Nationality:** Colombian + US citizen

## Background

- Grew up in Cali, Colombia; parents both entrepreneurs
- Left for Barcelona at 17-18; father died while he was in Europe
- Started first company at 20 in Madrid (iPod music curation service — "Spotify without the internet")
- Moved to NYC (2009) during financial crisis
- Built grocery e-commerce enabling Reliance Industries (India) — saw how mobile leapfrogging worked in emerging markets
- This led to founding Rappi (2015) in Bogotá, then immediately expanded to Mexico

## Mental Models / Beliefs

- **Defensibility above all:** "Durability is a function of defensibility." Focus on moats: network effects, scale, switching costs, hard IP, brand.
- **Run toward complexity:** Hard problems are better places to build because they're more defensible. Complexity = competitive moat.
- **Global talent, local focus:** Hire globally elite people (learned from immigrant experience); don't be bounded by what's locally considered "great."
- **Authentic founder:** Works best when doing things he genuinely cares about and with people he enjoys.
- **Humans as a Service (HaaS):** LATAM's economy was built on cheap labor instead of software. AI displaces this with something better.
- **AI sovereignty:** Concerned about China dominating LATAM's AI infrastructure stack (telco, cloud, applications). Wants American hemisphere integration.
- **Inside-out AI:** Enterprises need to see what agents are doing (Centre Pompidou metaphor). Black-box AI won't get enterprise adoption.

## Notable Quotes

> "I believe that if you build a system that allows companies to deal with all of this complexity, companies are going to be able to do things that were unheard of before."
