## Executive Summary

Winston Weinberg is the CEO and co-founder of Harvey, a legal AI platform. Former lawyer (practiced for ~1 year at a firm after law school). First-time founder. Known for being unusually direct about difficult topics — e.g., openly acknowledged Harvey will likely cause hiring freezes in law firms. Very focused on building defensible moats against LLM providers (the "sherlock defense" mindset).

## State

- **Company:** Harvey (CEO & Co-founder)

- **First-time founder**
- **Operating style:** Async-first, zero-out Slack every 20 min, direct communicator

## Beliefs / Mental Models

- Assume OpenAI/Anthropic will enter your vertical — always. Build moats against them, not just startups.
- Hire for "done this before at scale" vs. "high slope junior" is the hardest hiring decision in application-layer companies.
- Internal communication quality = external communication quality ceiling.
- Future of legal work: same hours, less burnout — AI eliminates monotonous tasks, not the job.
- Legal market is cyclical with the economy; as complexity increases, legal demand grows with it.
- "Navy Seals + Scale": companies need both high-skill nimble squads AND disciplined scaled systems simultaneously.

## Notable Quotes

> "Just assume they're going to directly go into your vertical. Just no matter what. Just assume that they're going to. How would you defend against that and on what time scale?"

> "My gut is there will be some hiring freezes. I don't think it will be cutting. It'll be some potential hiring freezes as they figure it out."

> "If you are thinking that you are going to make some company bets that prepare you for that long term... it might eat some short-term gains but it will help you long term."

> "In 10 years lawyers are going to do work exactly the same hours. But if they are doing work that they enjoy significantly more, they won't be as burned out."
