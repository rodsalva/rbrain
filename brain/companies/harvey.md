## Executive Summary

Harvey is a legal AI platform founded in 2022. Started as a productivity suite for lawyers at law firms; now expanded to large enterprise (Global 2000) customers. Making the platform "multiplayer"/collaborative. Long-term vision: transform how legal services — and adjacent professional services (tax, compliance) — are delivered. Raised Series D of $300M as of late 2025. CEO: Winston Weinberg (former lawyer, 1 year at a firm).

## State

- **CEO:** Winston Weinberg
- **Founded:** ~2022 (3 years old as of Nov 2025)
- **Stage:** Series D — $300M raised
- **Customers:** Law firms + Global 2000 enterprises
- **22% of users are partners** (high for enterprise software)
- **Verticals expanding into:** Tax and compliance (from legal core)
- **Key partnership:** Singapore Supreme Court (AI-assisted small claims filing)

## Product

- Productivity suite for lawyers: document review, M&A due diligence, research, drafting
- Platform becoming "multiplayer" — collaborative between law firms and their enterprise clients
- Mobile app in development (iOS-first; tablet is important channel for senior partners)
- Async task model: "send work like email to associate, get result back when done"
- Law school partnerships — building brand loyalty early (Apple/MacBook strategy)

## Business Model

- Enterprise SaaS + transitioning law firm clients to fixed-fee pricing for commoditized tasks
- Senior/partner work (client relationships, strategy) remains billable at premium
- Corporate clients using Harvey in-house and reducing external counsel spend

## Competitive Position

- Largest indirect competitors: OpenAI, Anthropic (LLM providers) — not other legaltech startups
- Strategy: build moats against foundation model providers, not just peers
- GTM deliberately targets large firms/enterprises to avoid the commoditizable lower end
- Enterprise clients have ChatGPT AND Harvey — co-existence validates the moat
