## Executive Summary

Rappi is the first delivery super app in Latin America and the first Colombian unicorn. Founded in 2015 by Sebastián Mejia, Felipe, and Simón. Operates across 9 countries, 300 cities. Delivers groceries, restaurants, and other goods — some cases under 10 minutes. Highly defensible: the only independent founder-led company in this category in the region. Co-founders Felipe and Simón continue to lead after Sebas stepped away.

## State

- **Founders:** Sebastián Mejia (departed ~2024), Felipe (co-founder, active), Simón (co-founder, active)
- **Scale:** 9 countries, 300 cities
- **Category:** Delivery super app / local commerce OS
- **Notable:** First Colombian unicorn; spawned 100-150+ companies from Rappi alumni

## Why Rappi Had to Build Everything from Scratch

Operating in Latin America meant no existing infrastructure stack:
- **Logistics:** No FedEx/USPS equivalent — had to build from zero
- **Payments:** Cash-driven markets; had to build cash acceptance capability
- **Fraud:** Western fraud detection tools couldn't identify LATAM-specific patterns; built own systems
- **The result:** Vertical integration is hard but highly defensible

## Strategic Lessons

- "Durability and defensibility are two things I think constantly about."
- Going vertical (owning logistics, payments, fraud) is harder but creates moats
- Expanding internationally fast (2 months in → Mexico) against conventional VC advice was right
- Being battlehardened operators in a low-margin, competitive, multi-vertical business built capability
