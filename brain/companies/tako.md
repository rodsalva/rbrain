## Executive Summary

Tako (stylized "Taco" internally) is an AI-powered intelligence layer for workforce operations, founded by Sebastián "Sebas" Mejia (formerly founder of Rappi, first Colombian unicorn). The company builds agents that understand all of Brazil's labor regulation and provides applications to manage HR operations end-to-end. Primary focus: absorbing the regulatory complexity of Brazil — the most complex labor jurisdiction in the world — so companies don't have to.

## State

- **CEO:** Sebastián "Sebas" Mejia
- **Co-founder:** Fernando (Brazilian co-founder)
- **Headquarters:** Brazil + San Francisco hacker house
- **Stage:** Early (building and scaling across Brazil, expanding to other LATAM markets)
- **Customers:** Medium-sized to large enterprises in Brazil (tens of thousands of employees)
- **Differentiator:** Only company with a complete, machine-readable corpus of all Brazilian labor law

## Products

### Auto
Legal intelligence for labor compliance. Benchmarks better than general LLMs on Brazilian labor law. Helps companies understand if they're staying compliant. Used by companies where non-compliance means fines, back-payments, lawsuits.

### Hiring / Contratação
Agents that handle recruitment and onboarding end-to-end, integrated with eSocial. Can onboard a company in days once eSocial access is granted.

### Labor Events (Férias, Termination)
Automates all HR lifecycle events driven by Brazilian labor law: vacations, terminations, government filings. Critical because Brazilian termination law is complex — get it wrong and you get sued.

### Enterprise
Goes to largest organizations to solve hardest HR/people problems. Builds a people database that agents can operate across, with a proprietary agent OS that handles tool selection, model selection, telemetry, and usage tracking.

## Strategic Thesis

> "I believe that if you build a system that allows companies to deal with all of this complexity, companies are going to be able to do things that were unheard of before."

- Complexity = defensibility. Hard problems are the best place to build durable companies.
- Tako absorbs liability: "I'm absorbing all of that liability because the company is saying you're going to manage this for me."
- Inside-out AI transparency: inspired by Centre Pompidou (Paris) — users should see the pipes, the tools, the models, the progress. Enterprise adoption requires visibility into what agents are doing.
- Brazil first, then expand: if you solve Brazil (hardest), other LATAM markets are easier.

## Technical Foundation

- Complete dataset: all corpus of Brazilian labor law in code form
- Proper taxonomy of how laws interact with each other
- Data pipelines to stay current as laws change
- eSocial integration (10M companies, 60M employee records)
- Proprietary agent OS: tool selection, model selection, evaluations, telemetry
